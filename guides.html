    • Nuwera Eliya

    Nuwera Eliya is a city in the Central province of Sri Lanka that is filled with many tourist attractions. Let me suggest travel ideas and places to visit when we travel via the Peradeniya (A5 route). After passing Gampola, we can feel the cool climate and upon passing Pussellawa we can encounter tea estates. As a refreshing stop for our trip, we can use ‘Appachige Kade’, where most of the travelers use this shop for breakfast and refreshments. Beyond Tawalantenne, we can feel the cool climate very well accompanied by many curvy roads. We can catch distant views of Gerandi Ella, Puna Falls and after passing Puna Falls viewpoint, we’ll encounter Ramboda Falls and we can travel through Ramboda Cave. After passing Ramboda Cave, there are main two tea estates that travelers usually visit; ‘Blue Field’ tea factory and ‘Damro’ tea plantations. Usually, many of the travelers use ‘Damro’ viewpoint to get photos. After passing ‘Damro’ tea plantation, we can enter to Nuwara Eliya. Now, let’s explore each tourist attraction in Nuwara Eliya one by one. These destinations include budget friendly places for University students.
    1. Shanthipura

    Shanthipura is considered to be the highest village in the North East region of Sri Lanka and can be accessed from the Nuwara Eliya Kandy road. Situated at an altitude of 7339 ft on the stunning Kikiliyamana Mountain road from Nuwara Eliya and at the base of a hill, Shanthipura is a little village that offers a panoramic view of the serene blue hills and the lake adjacent to the valley. The landscape gradually ascends, with lush tea bushes. In Shanthipura, there is a viewpoint and for the local visitors, the ticket cost will be 50/100 LKR.
    2. Nuwera Eliya Post Office

    Built in the year 1894, the Nuwara Eliya Post Office building exhibits a Tudor-era design, in keeping the architectural style of many of the other colonial buildings within the town. Complete with a red brick exterior and a clock tower spiraling from the corner of the building, the building showcases a glimpse of old-world Ceylon and serves as a major tourist attraction in ‘Little England’
    3. Gregory lake

    This is a prominent attraction used for water sports and recreational activities. Lake Park, Boat Rides are available. At the southern end of the town is Gregory Lake where the holiday makers can enjoy a leisurely walk on the paved walk ways, with swan shaped paddle boats for the more adventurous. There are pony rides around the lake for the kids and even adults, with plenty of fast food stalls located in a separate area where one can enjoy a meal or a snack. Ticket cost for Gregory Lake is 200LKR
    4. Loversleap Waterfall

    Lovers leap is an impressive water fall which is known for its romantic folklore. To reach this location, one has to go through the tea factory and reach the tea manager’s bungalow along a tough path. Visitors can reach this destination by following the signs that are there and eventually one can reach a rock face and a Shiva shrine. Just beyond the shrine one can see the cascading water fall. Legend has it that lovers have leapt to their deaths from this water fall and hence the name Lovers Leap. Loversleap waterfalls is 5.5Km away from Nuwera eliya and it is situated in hawa eliya.
    5. Moons Plains

    Moon plains is a 400 meter long and 30 - 40 meter deep valley, till the late 1990s and was part of a catchment area. Once you go to this location, the scenery is so beautiful, that you can feel that you are in a different world. Located in Nuwara Eliya is this Agricultural and Environment zone which was opened in March 2014. This beautiful area of land is made more picturesque by its flat and slightly slopy landmass with the blue hills in the background. One can see wild deer, elk, wild buffalo grazing on the green plains and many endemic bird varieties. Moon Plains is a mini Worlds End and at its edge gives a 3560 degree view of the surrounding peaks and towers. If one is lucky, you may see a leopard or two. Moons plain is located 5.7Km far away from Nuwara Eliya in Ranhawadigama Rd, Nuwara Eliya. For safari truck it cost 3000LKR and we can use it for 2 hours.
    6. Single Tree Hills

    The word “Single Tree” has been in existence from time immemorial. An excellent view of the sun rise can be seen from the top of this mountain. Situated 6890 ft above the sea level, it takes about 90 minutes to reach the top. Single Tree hill is the best vantage point to observe the beauty of Nuwara Eliya and the beauty of Haggala Mountain range. Single Tree Mountain unravels the spectacular views of the entire Nuwara Eliya Town including other mountains such as Piduruthalagala and Haggala mountains. After trekking through tea plantations, one reaches the top where there is just 1 single tree. This is the 7th highest mountain in Sri Lanka and is easily accessible. If one gets to the top around 5.30 a.m. – 7.00 a.m. one can see the best cloud formations around Sri Lanka and also the reflection of the mountain through Gregory Lake. On a very clear day one can see Piduruthalagala Mountain (Pedro Mountain), Lovers leap falls, Lake Gregory and urban areas of Nuwara Eliya in the northern valley and the southern valley of Adam’s Peak, Seven Virgin Hills, conical Hill and UdaRadella mountains.It is located 4.2Km far from Nuwara Eliya.
    7. Hakgala Botanical Garden

    Haggala Botanical Garden is one of the five botanical gardens in Sri Lanka. The other four are Peradeniya Botanical Garden, Henarathgoda Botanical Garden, Mirijjawila Botanical Garden and Seetawaka Botanical Garden. It is the second largest botanical garden in Sri Lanka. The garden is contiguous to Haggala Strict Nature Reserve. Haggala Botanical Garden is situated in the Nuwara Eliya - Badulla main road, 16 km from Nuwara Eliya and extents for about 28 hectares.
    8. Bomburu Ella Waterfall

    After visiting to Haggala botanical garden, we need to move along Badulla road and we come across a junction near Haggala muslim mosque and have to take a left turn and move 8 km to reach Bomburu Ella. Bomburu Ella is a nice travelling spot for those who wish to do tracking. From parking there is 1.5km until waterfall. Ticket per person is 50LKR. Until we reach waterfall there are many villagers who provide plain tea, rotti and other beverages. Bomburu Ella is a nice place to get photos and to take a bath.


    And these are some places to view within Nuwara Eliya and when we return, we need to choose the Nanu Oya to Talawakele route (A7 road) to see some more beautiful places. You can find a brief description about those destinations below.
    9. Naanu Oya Falls

    Nanu Oya Waterfall is situated in Nanu Oya in the Nuwara Eliya district, just 6 km away from Nuwara Eliya. It stands 60 meters high, cascading down a sequence of 25 steps. The falls join with the Kotmale and Mahaweli rivers, creating a very scenic view of the nature. The water of Nanu Oya Waterfall flows through the Udaradella and Bambarakelle areas, starting from the Nanu Oya (River) and reaching the fall. Nanu Oya Waterfall can be seen from the main road and is a beautiful waterfall in Sri Lanka. Take the road by the railway station and travel about 1.5 km on this route to reach Nanu Oya Waterfall. After viewing Nanu Oya falls we can reach Talawakale. The road which leads to Talawakale is slippery and curvy road in middle of tea estate. To Talawakale from Nanu Oya we need to travel 15kms
    10. St Clairs Falls

    St. Clair's fall is one of the widest waterfalls in Sri Lanka and is commonly known as the "Little Niagara” of Sri Lanka. It is one of the six waterfalls affected by the Upper Kotmale Hydropower Project. The falls are situated 3 km (1.9 mi) west of the town of Talawakale on the Hatton-Talawakale Highway in Nuwara Eliya district. We can see the waterfall from the road itself. And if we need to get closer photos we can travel along the tea estate and get photos.
    11. Devons Falls

    Devon Falls is situated near the Hatton-Nuwara Eliya main road. The waterfall is approximately 4 km from St. Clair’s waterfall. It belongs to the Nuwara Eliya district of Sri Lanka. The height of the fall is 97 meters. It is considered to be the 19th tallest waterfall in the country. There is a nice viewpoint to watch the beauty of the waterfall in the Hatton -Nuwara Eliya road. You can sit there and observe the waterfall very clearly. The place is also nice for taking photographs. Mlesta Tea Castle is near the viewpoint. So, you can go there and enjoy a cup of tea. The viewpoint is comprised of enough spaces to park your vehicles. This is the place where most travelers capture photographs of the waterfall. There is no other place to appreciate the beauty of this waterfall. For the viewpoint, we need to travel 500 meters via kovil.

    And after viewing Devon falls, we don’t need to visit Hatton to reach Nawalapitiya. We can use Pathana route. This route gives us some beautiful experiences which is surrounded by tea estates. And by choosing this route, we can visit Kadiyanlena waterfall near the road.
    12. Kadiyanlena Ella Falls

    The water of the 8m-high Kadiyanlena Ella Falls cascades down in stages. Although the flow is otherwise languid, during rain the fall is transformed into a rushing body of water, the spray reaching the nearby road. This is a beautiful waterfall where we can take many beautiful photographs.
